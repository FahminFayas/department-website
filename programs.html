Program Overview

The Bachelor of Computer Applications (BCA) is a three-year undergraduate program designed to provide students with a strong foundation in computer science and applications. The program covers various aspects of computing, including programming, software development, database management, and more.

Courses Offered

  • Introduction to Programming
  • Data Structures and Algorithms
  • Database Management Systems
  • Web Development
  • Operating Systems
  • Software Engineering
  • Computer Networks

Admissions

To apply for the BCA program, you typically need a high school diploma or equivalent with a background in mathematics. Admission requirements may vary by institution, so be sure to check the specific requirements of the colleges or universities you are interested in.
